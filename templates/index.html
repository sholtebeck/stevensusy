  1. Ala Moana Hotel - Business class hotel near Magic Island & Ala Moana Shopping Center
  2. Aqua Palms Waikiki - All the Aqua properties are nice and affordable; this one is convenient to Magic Island, Ala Moana and Waikiki
  3. Hale Koa Hotel - Military hotel near Fort DeRussy Beach. Available only to active duty, veteran, or surviving family of the armed/uniformed services.
  4. Surfjack Hotel & Swim Club - Boutique suite (sweet) hotel in Waikiki
  5. Try short term rentals on VRBO or Airbnb.
  1. Chuck's Cellar - I've (Steve) been eating here since I was a wee kid. A Holtebeck family tradition.
  2. Eggs 'n Things - Popular breakfast/brunch place with three locations around Waikiki.
  3. Marukame Udon - Choose your broth, noodles & toppings. Worth the wait in their perpetual line.
  4. Rainbow Drive-In - Famous Hawaiian plate lunch, near the edge of Waikiki.
  5. Shirokiya Japan Village Walk - huge Hong Kong-style food court at Ala Moana Mall.
      Sushi, udon, wagyu, bento, burgers, pizza, spam musubi, okonomiyaki, and $1 beers (if you’re not too snobbish). Plus a
      Guardian Spirits Sanctuary where you can seek your good luck guide among 8 different buddhas and 12 animal spirits. What more could you want?

  1. Malasadas from Leonard's Bakery - think warm jelly doughnuts without the jelly (or regular ones without the hole).
      We like the plain cinnamon sugar ones, but if you like filling do it up with vanilla, chocolate, or coconut(haupia).
  2. Shave Ice from Uncle Clay’s House of Pure Aloha (Susy’s favorite), but any of the dozens of stands around Waikiki are ono if you don't have a car.
  3. Darn good ice cream from Lappert’s in Hilton Hawaiian Village.
  4. Diamondhead puff or any of the other fabulous and beautiful treats at Kulu Kulu, the bakery in Shirokiya (see dining, above)
  5. If prefer to drink your treats, we recommend Beer Lab HI, Honolulu Beer Works, The Brewseum, or Waikiki Brewing Company.

  1. Diamond Head State Monument - A nice long paved walk up the crater. There are a couple steep staircases and one spot where
      you need to bend over nearly double to get through an old sentry bunker, but it’s a fun, easy climb. Just East of Waikiki.
  2. Makapuʻu Point Lighthouse Trail - Another nice hike, just East of Diamond Head.
  3. Manoa Falls - A shady, pretty, 1.5 mile hike to a waterfall, past the U of H campus one valley over from our place. Often rainy/muddy so be prepared!
  4. Kapiolani Regional Park - Pretty area at the east end of Waikiki. Adjacent to the zoo, aquarium, and Sans Souci Beach.
  5. Ala Wai Golf Course - Grab your clubs or rent theirs, and walk this public course right across the canal from Waikiki.
  1. Iolani Palace - The only royal residence on US soil, home to the Kings and Queens of Hawai’i from the Kamehameha Dynasty
      until the monarchy was overthrown and the Islands annexed by the US in the late 19th century
  2. Pearl Harbor - This is on the West side of Honolulu near the airport, so it's a good thing to do right after arriving or right before leaving.
  3. Puu Ualakaa State Park - Fantastic views over Honolulu and Waikiki. Tantalus Lookout is just up the hill from our place
  4. Hanauma Bay Nature Preserve - Say hi to the sea turtles for us!
  5. Head to the beach. Any beach, but the closest is probably going to be the famous Waikiki Beach.
